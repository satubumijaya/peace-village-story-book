INTRODUCTION

On Indonesia’s island of Java, women are essential to overcoming the threat posed by radicalism towards peace and human security at community level. Everyday women – diverse in age and background – are flourishing to become agents for peace, leading their communities to prevent violence, promote tolerance, and improve social cohesion.

These true grassroots leaders navigate deeply rooted patriarchy and gender conservatism, working towards community resilience, equality for all citizens, and real respect for diversity within each of their villages – known as Peace Villages

In the working group itself there are approximately 40% of members who are male, and they remain accepting – and enthusiastic – of such women-led movements in a traditionally patriarchal society. For Hasanah, as a life-long activist, the impact of the Peace Village programme has significant personal meaning, as herself and other women now feel more fulfilled and engaged than ever before.

They then sit with their local government to explore and identify ways to promote tolerance and sustain peace in their village, and push for change in village government policy and processes. This approach, through both community and government, counters the issues of radicalism and intolerance from both ends, fostering understanding, resilience and peaceful enjoyment of life in the village for all.

In particular, it is four Peace Villages in East Java that are piloting approaches established on global human security principles, while maintaining a focus towards resilience within the community.

Supported by UN Women and the Wahid Foundation under the Guyub Project, the villages of Prancak, Sidomulyo, Candirenggo and Guluk-Guluk evidence strong outcomes and impacts stemming from the empowerment and inclusion of women.

Peace Village Action Plans form the foundation of progress in these particular villages, with women leading and contributing to plan development and implementation in their communities. It is the stories of empowerment, inclusion and leadership for the women themselves – agents for peace and promoters of diversity – that truly reflect the meaning and importance of the Indonesian Peace Village.

GULUK-GULUK

VILLAGE

Located in the Sumenep region on Madura Island, Guluk-Guluk village was traditionally known for ongoing community conflict and tensions, often related to local politics.

Through the Peace Village programme, women were engaged in economic empowerment activities that increased their standing and voice in their community, and resulted in successful peacebuilding and conflict resolution efforts led by women in the community.

Women – young and old – are now approaching the village’s political conflicts head-on, and ensuring harmonious and peaceful relationships between all parties throughout Guluk-Guluk village.

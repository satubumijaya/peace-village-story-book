PRANCAK

VILLAGE

For women in Prancak village, in Madura island’s Sumenep region, patriarchy has often hindered their engagement in social and political life.

The establishment of strong women’s groups through the Peace Village programme formed a key driver for change, supporting women to take on increasing responsibility and leadership across Prancak village and its institutions.

Now, women take leading roles in their neighbourhoods and village groups, working towards ending domestic violence and increasing protection for women and children in Prancak.

SIDOMULYO

VILLAGE

On the outskirts of Batu City, Sidomulyo village has often faced issues for women and children related to poverty and low levels of education for many of its residents.

By engaging women and youth, the Peace Village programme focused towards directly protecting women and children, while also approaching the causes of community conflict, including working with households to generate income rather than taking loans.

Women are central to the Peace Village efforts that include income development training, as well as protection and improvement of education opportunities for Sidomulyo’s children.

CANDIRENGGO

VILLAGE

Just north of Malang City, Candirenggo village has been constantly challenged to balance its diversity with its citizen’s more traditional beliefs and practices.

To counter an increase in conflict and disharmony over recent years, the Peace Village programme has empowered women in the community to become leaders and drivers of peace and tolerance at the village level.

Women’s groups have developed safe spaces for women to have a more profound voice in village affairs, while youth have also become more central to peacebuilding efforts, resulting in a more inclusive and harmonious environment for all community members in Candirenggo.

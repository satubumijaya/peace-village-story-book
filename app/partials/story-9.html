09. HALIMAH

“I just want our community to live together
– in peace.”

Halimah joined a local neighbourhood women’s group to be more involved outside of her home in the community, and to take part in livelihood training which could support her small cake business. The group began as a space to learn about opportunities for improving economic outcomes, and has transformed into a place for the women of Candirenggo village, such as Halimah, to play an active role in supporting and developing their wider community.

“Most of us have traditionally just worked taking care of our homes, so I really wanted to be part of something more. I wanted to grow.”

Initial activities in the Karya Wiguna Gunda women’s group focused on handicraft, sewing and cooking.

Halimah recognised the value of such an opportunity for women to increase their contribution to their household finances.

Economic empowerment was only the beginning for the group, with the women then approaching other community challenges.

They now approach domestic violence and other household issues through their tight-knit group through support from the Peace Village programme.

“Sometimes there can be a lot of problems between women in the village. But our women’s group now gathers often, and we know each other better, so conflict between women has decreased.”

The group provides Halimah and others a space in which to have a voice, and also a place to turn if things get difficult. Previously, issues for women could often be amplified and protracted as news leaked out into the wider community.

The establishment of the women’s group through the Peace Village programme countered this by providing direct and relevant support for whatever problems may arise. The group also gives victims of domestic violence somewhere to turn for help, and Halimah and her friends are ready to support.

“So many of the women have increased self-confidence. We are always invited to activities and discussions in the village now, as our input is valued, and we have the confidence to deliver our ideas in front of everyone.”

More than just a training and support mechanism, the group is now actively engaged on shaping outcomes for Halimah and other women in her neighbourhood. They are increasingly taking part in village planning and decision-making activities, and the impact of this on the women of Candirenggo is significant.

As for Halimah, her hopes are simple – for her, her family and the community of Candirenggo.

“We hope to just live in peace with our families, without the problem of domestic violence. And even if there are problems, we want to deal with them properly to avoid further issues.”
